You will build the stack, contribute to our shared vision, and connect with a diverse community of spatial web developers.

The technical stuff

Let's get the technical stuff out of the way. We obviously need you to be a great engineer!

We need somebody who has:

  1. Experience building low latency collaborative software
  2. Experience with AWS, Google Cloud, Firebase, or similar
  3. Built full-stack services, with especially strong systems and backends knowledge
  4. Familiarity with OpenGL, WebGL, WebXR, or THREE.js
  5. Game development or adjacent 3D software experience a plus

We are not going to count how many months or years you spent building for each technologies, but we will give you exercises to verify your approach and your code quality.

The important, non-technical stuff

There are lots of non-technical things we also expect you to be great at:

  1. As the third person on the team, you will contribute significantly in building the culture of the company. How you react to feedback, how you give kudos to people, or how you voice a disagreement will have vital importance in defining our culture.
  2. We value curiosity. Mixed reality is a space that still has so much to explore. Nobody knows how people will interact with mixed reality in 10 years. Let’s figure it out together.
  3. We value transparency. We build in public, and we’re not shy about showing the bad first sketch. We would like you to embrace the spirit of building in public too.

What would you say… you do here?

Here are the things you will own, and the things you will assist with.

You will own
  1. The backend architecture
  2. The external documentation, and will work with us to make it a great developer experience
  3. Testing and QA: the processes to insure the quality and robustness of our platform
  4. Work with Laurent to integrate people’s feedback into our release cycle
You will assist with
  1. Defining the architecture with Michael
  2. Collaborate with us on the frontend
  3. Defining the product by participating in brainstorming sessions and workshops
  4. Reviewing user experience flows and give feedback
  5. Defining and maintaining MR.js, the open-source library Volumetrics is built upon

Your first month at Volumetrics

During your first month, you will spend time with Michael and Laurent to form as a team, familiarize yourself with our mission, our strategy, and contribute to our high-level vision.

You will define the right architecture for our back-end, select the right vendors, and work with us to define the roadmap, features, plan and prioritization.

Your first year at Volumetrics

During your first year, you will launch multiple releases of both MR.js and Volumetrics. You will:

  • strategize with us on the product roadmap
  • write a lot of code, both front and backend
  • assist with the product launches
  • help early adopters to learn and become proficient with MR.js
  • write kick-ass interactive documentation
  • help grow our internal and external communities

The money stuff

We are commited to transparency, and that includes compensation. With that in mind, here are the numbers:

Salary & Equity
  • $140k USD to $160k USD annually, paid biweekly
  • 0.5% to 2% equity, based on your salary requirements
Benefits
  • You get 3 weeks of mandatory vacation
  • Healthcare benefits: Medical, Dental, and Vision

On top of this, you get good, modern hardware:

  • A work headset, provided to you
  • A work laptop of your choice, up to $2500
  • A $500 yearly stipend to equipment, health or fitness

Location and hours

Both Michael and Laurent are in the Bay Area, and we meet weekly around Redwood City.

We would love to be able to meet weekly in person with you too, but if you think you are a perfect fit except for the distance, please still apply!

We expect you to work 40 hours a week. We expect you to deliver consistently, at a decent pace, without burning yourself out. We don’t a have strict schedule. However, we meet synchronously every morning, and connect adhoc throughout the day.

Ready to apply?

The process will be as follow:

  1. Send us an email containing a link to your website, git repos, and/or a resume as PDF
  2. If you'd like, something that captures your personality, from a joke, to a side project
  3. If we'd like to move forwards, we'll reach out for a chat
  4. You will speak individually to both Michael and Laurent
  5. You may be asked to write some code, as an exercise

Regardless, you will hear from us in 48h, and we're excited to hear from you!

Send us an email!

If you have any question, feel free to reach out via email, or directly to Michael or Laurent on Mastodon.
